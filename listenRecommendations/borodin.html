Movement III, Notturno from Borodin's String Quartet no.2 (1881).

I've always been a fan of strong Russian music and this certainly is an example of simply put, a really well composed piece. It really doesn't need a lengthy written explanation, listening to a performance of it describes more than words ever can. The theme on its own is so ridiculously lyrical and romantic. It transfers across various voices and goes through thematic development without the phrasing ever feeling any less seamless than a bird gliding through clouds. When I listen to this, I envision a wintery landscape with some animated animal characters. If there is ever to be another Fantasia film, I think this piece would be a fantastic choice to animate a story around. There is such lyricism in every note.
